Privacy Policy

How we collect, use, and protect your personal information

Last updated: December 2024

Our Commitment to Your Privacy

RAMA ADVISORY is committed to protecting your privacy and handling your personal information in accordance with the Privacy Act 1988 (Cth) and the Australian Privacy Principles (APPs). This Privacy Policy explains how we collect, use, disclose, and store your personal information.

1. Information We Collect

Personal Information

  • Contact details (name, address, phone, email)
  • Financial information (income, expenses, assets, liabilities)
  • Employment details and history
  • Identification documents (driver's license, passport)
  • Credit history and credit-related information
  • Property and loan preferences

2. How We Collect Information

Directly From You

  • Application forms
  • Phone conversations
  • Email correspondence
  • Face-to-face meetings
  • Website forms

From Third Parties

  • Credit reporting bodies
  • Lenders and financial institutions
  • Employers (with consent)
  • Government agencies
  • Professional referrers

3. How We Use Your Information

  • Assess your loan application and creditworthiness
  • Provide mortgage broking and financial services
  • Communicate with lenders on your behalf
  • Comply with legal and regulatory requirements
  • Provide ongoing customer service and support
  • Send you relevant product information (with consent)
  • Improve our services and website functionality

4. Information Disclosure

We may disclose your personal information to:

Financial Services

  • Banks and lenders
  • Credit reporting bodies
  • Mortgage insurers
  • Valuers and settlement agents

Professional Services

  • Legal and accounting professionals
  • IT service providers
  • Document storage providers
  • Government agencies (when required)

5. Data Security

We implement appropriate security measures to protect your personal information:

  • Secure storage systems with access controls
  • Encrypted data transmission
  • Regular security assessments and updates
  • Staff training on privacy and security
  • Secure disposal of documents when no longer needed

6. Your Rights

Under Australian privacy law, you have the right to:

  • Access your personal information we hold
  • Request correction of inaccurate information
  • Make a complaint about our privacy practices
  • Request we stop sending marketing communications
  • Understand how your information is being used

7. Credit Reporting

We may collect credit information from and disclose credit information to credit reporting bodies. This includes:

  • Credit applications and credit history
  • Repayment history information
  • Default information (if applicable)
  • Court judgments and bankruptcy information

You can request not to receive direct marketing from credit reporting bodies by contacting them directly.

8. Website and Cookies

Our website may use cookies and similar technologies to:

  • Improve website functionality and user experience
  • Analyze website traffic and usage patterns
  • Remember your preferences and settings
  • Provide relevant content and advertisements

You can control cookie settings through your browser preferences.

If you have concerns about our privacy practices or wish to make a complaint:
